# Incident Report — False Positive Email Tuning

## Incident ID
INC-20260613-01

## Executive Summary
A SIEM email alert triggered on 13 June 2026 for an inbound message from `leonard@fashionindustrytrends.xyz`. The email contained no attachments, no active URLs, and behavior consistent with unsolicited marketing spam. The incident was reviewed and closed as a false positive, with recommendations to tune detection logic.

## Findings
- Sender: `leonard@fashionindustrytrends.xyz`
- Recipient: `yani.zubair@tryhatme.com`
- Trigger: heuristic on `.xyz` TLD
- No payload, links, or phishing indicators present
- Raw content aligned with generic marketing copy

## Analysis
The alert was generated by a TLD-based heuristic without sufficient corroborating risk indicators. The absence of malicious artifacts or credential-harvesting behavior supports a false positive classification.

## Recommendations
- Refine SIEM rules so unusual TLDs are a contributing signal rather than a single escalation criterion
- Require additional conditions such as SPF/DKIM failures, low domain age, or bad reputation before alerting
- Add `fashionindustrytrends.xyz` to the gateway UBE/quarantine policy for repeated unsolicited traffic
- Monitor for future alerts from the same sender or domain and adjust detection tuning based on volume and behavior

## Closure
The incident is closed with documented rationale: false positive due to marketing content and lack of malicious indicators. This report should be retained for tuning feedback and future email detection reviews.
